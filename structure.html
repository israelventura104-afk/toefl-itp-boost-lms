Structure and Written Expression

Learn the patterns before you answer the questions.

In this section, do not guess by what “sounds right.” Train your eyes to find the structure: subject, verb, connector, and error pattern.

Free preview

Three strategies everyone can study first.

01

Find the subject and the real verb first.

A TOEFL Structure question is like a sentence with missing bones. Before looking at the answer choices, ask: “Who or what is the sentence about?” That is the subject. Then ask: “What action or state belongs to that subject?” That is the main verb.

Example

The students in the laboratory ___ the results carefully.

Think: The subject is not “laboratory.” The subject is “students.” The verb must match a plural subject.

Best answer: analyze

02

Do not confuse connectors with prepositions.

Some words need a complete sentence after them. Other words only need a noun phrase. This is one of the easiest traps to catch if you slow down and classify the word.

Although / While / Because Need subject + verb
Despite / During / Because of Need noun phrase, not a full sentence
Simple check

Although the test was difficult = correct.

Despite the difficult test = correct.

03

Ignore extra information between commas.

Written Expression often hides the real subject by adding extra information in the middle. If a phrase appears between commas, cover it mentally. Then connect the subject directly with the verb.

Example

The professor, along with two assistants, are preparing the exam.

Remove the middle: The professor are preparing...

Now it is obvious: “are” should be “is.”

Listening Comprehension

Listen for meaning, not for repeated words.

In TOEFL ITP Listening, the correct answer usually says the same idea with different words. Train your ear to catch meaning, attitude, and intention.

Free preview

Three listening strategies everyone can use.

01

Pay special attention to the second speaker.

In short conversations, the first speaker often gives the situation. The second speaker usually gives the opinion, problem, correction, or final meaning. If you get lost, hold on to the second voice.

Example

Speaker A: Are you going to the meeting?

Speaker B: I wish I could, but I have a lab report due.

Meaning: The second speaker is not going. Do not choose an answer just because it mentions "meeting."

02

Be careful with answers that repeat exact words.

TOEFL loves distractors. If an answer repeats a word you clearly heard, do not trust it immediately. The correct answer often uses a synonym or paraphrase.

You hear "The exam was exhausting."
You look for "The test was very tiring."
Simple check

Same word does not always mean correct answer. Same meaning is what matters.

03

Follow long conversations with your eyes.

In longer conversations and talks, the questions usually follow the order of the audio. Move your eyes through the options while listening. When you hear a detail, date, place, or reason, connect it to the next question.

Teacher move

Do not stare at one question for too long. If the audio has moved on, you move on too.

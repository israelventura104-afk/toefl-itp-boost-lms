Reading Comprehension

Read with a mission, not with panic.

TOEFL ITP Reading rewards strategic searching. You do not need to understand every word first. You need to know what each question is asking you to find.

Free preview

Three reading strategies everyone can use.

01

For main idea, read the beginning first.

Main idea questions usually depend on the opening lines of the passage or paragraph. Do not start by reading every word slowly. First, scan the first sentences and ask: "What is this mainly about?"

Simple method

Read the first two lines. Check the answer choices. Eliminate choices that are too specific or too extreme.

02

For vocabulary, read around the word.

Do not panic if you do not know the target word. Read the sentence before and the sentence after. The context usually tells you if the word is positive, negative, neutral, a problem, or a solution.

Do not ask only"What does this word mean in the dictionary?"
Ask instead"What does this word mean in this sentence?"
03

For pronoun reference, look backward.

When the question asks what "it," "they," or "which" refers to, the answer is usually before the pronoun, not after it. Move one sentence back and find the closest noun that matches number and meaning.

Quick check

If the pronoun is "they," your answer should probably be plural. If the pronoun is "it," your answer should probably be singular.
